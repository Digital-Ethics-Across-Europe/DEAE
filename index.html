Digital Ethics Across Europe

Navigating LGBTQ+ Discrimination Across European Borders

Digital Ethics Across Europe (DEAE) is an end-of-course project for the exam Open Access and Digital Ethics

We aim to analyze and repurpose open access datasets to highlight the significance of digital ethics in addressing LGBTQ+ disparities and to explore the variations in treatment across different European countries.

While we aim to present a balanced comparison, we recognize that no scholarly project is entirely free from bias and we acknowledge the limitations in our approach

Scenario

Discrimination against LGBTQ+ individuals remains a pressing issue across Europe, affecting fundamental aspects of life such as access to healthcare, employment opportunities, education, and social inclusion.

This project seeks to investigate and analyze the disparities faced by LGBTQ+ communities in different European countries through the use of open data. By collecting and examining datasets across various domains, the project aims to uncover patterns of inequality and provide insights into how these challenges vary geographically.

A quality analysis of our source datasets was performed following the Italian National Guidelines ("Linee guida nazionali per la valorizzazione del patrimonio informativo pubblico")

Four factors have been taken into consideration when analysing data quality:

  • Completeness, both of the records and the population
  • Accuracy, both syntactic and semantic
  • Coherence, so the presence of contradictions
  • Timeliness, which concerns when the data was last updated and the avarage update frequency

Visualisation

Various graphic representations have been employed to analyze and illustrate key aspects related to the experiences of the LGBTI community in Europe. These visualizations focus on multiple dimensions, including:

  • Levels of Discrimination perceived by members of the community in different areas of life
  • Attitudes of the general public towards LGBTI+ individuals
  • Legal status and rights associated with gay marriage, cohabitation, adoption, and other issues

Bar charts created using Charts.js have been used to present the data on discrimination, while a map shows how the legal status of gay people has changed during the years.

Discrimination and public opinion

2023

The following interactive graphs display relevant information regarding the treatment and experiences of LGBTQ+ people across the European Union. The first graph illustrates the percentage of discrimination experienced by LGBTQ+ people in 2023, broken down into three key areas of life: employment, healthcare, and education. The second graph presents the public opinion of EU citizens on issues affecting the LGBTQ+ community.

By comparing the two charts, you can observe disparities between the actual discrimination faced by the community and the public's perception of these issues. To explore the different topics represented in the charts, click on the bars below each chart and select one of the options.

Marriage and Adoption

1989 - 2024

This map shows data from two different resources, Rainbow Europe and The Laws and Families dataset. The datasets focus on legal family diversity, exploring marriage, registered partnerships, cohabitation, and adoption rights for both same-sex and different-sex couples, providing a legal framework for understanding family laws across Europe.

The Laws and Families dataset offers information on 20 European countries, from 1989 to 2015, while Rainbow Europe provides data for the present year. This leaves a gap in information between 2016 and 2024, highlighting the need for significant research and updates in this field.

Sustainability of the Open Data Analysis

LawsAndFamilies Database

The database covers 50 years of legal development, focusing on 69 issues, in 21 European countries; it is free and available to access online, enabling researchers, advocacy groups, and policymakers, to utilise this data effectively.

Despite this however, some issues arise regarding its relevance. Whilst the dataset does provide a historical account, it has not been updated since 2016. This places limitations on its timeliness and ongoing relevance.

To bridge this gap in our project we utilised the Rainbow Europe Map as it provides data from 2024 on 49 European countries.

Rainbow Europe Map

When evaluating the dataset's sustainability, we can see it is very sustainable as it updates annually, providing a timeliness account of each country's percentage that monitors current progressions and highlights areas that require attention.

However, as the Rainbow Map focuses on Legislation, it lacks grassroots-level data, including factors such as LGBTQ+ lived realities and experiences. To bridge this gap we chose to include LGBTQ+ surveys.

Discrimination Survey (Eurobarometer)

The Eurobarometer Discrimination Survey is sustainable as it is conducted frequently, providing an up-to-date evaluation of societal attitudes towards LGBTQ+ realities.

It is free and available to access online, enabling researchers, advocacy groups, and policymakers, to utilise this data effectively. It also promotes greater transparency and accountability regarding LGBTQ+ issues, enabling groups such as NGOs, government bodies, and academics to utilise this data in investigating, addressing, and mitigating these challenges.

LGBTIQ Survey III

The EU LGBTIQ Survey III is free and available to access online, providing greater transparency into the lived realities of LGBTQ+ individuals in Europe.

The dataset could be more sustainable if it was conducted more frequently, as previous surveys were conducted in 2012 and 2019. This would allow for greater insights into the rapidly changing social and political realities of LGBTQ+ individuals and communities.
